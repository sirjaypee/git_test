TUWO AND OKRO SOUP

Description

Savory Nigerian comfort: Tuwo, a fluffy cassava dough dipped in rich, spicy okra soup bursting with tomatoes, veggies, and protein - a flavor fiesta!

Ingrediants

    Tuwo:

  • 2 cups cassava flour (purchased pre-made or make your own by grinding dried, peeled cassava)
  • 1 cup of boiling water
  • Salt to taste

    • Okro soup

  • 1 table spoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • scotch bonnet pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 1 pound assorted meat (beef, chicken, goat, etc.), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 (400g) can okra, drained
  • 1 (400g) can chopped tomatoes
  • 1 cup tomato paste
  • cup water (or use meat broth for extra flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

    Tuwo:

  1. Bring water to a boil: In a pot, bring the water to a rolling boil.
  2. Add cassava flour gradually: Slowly whisk in the cassava flour while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Aim for a thick porridge-like consistency.
  3. Season and cook: Add salt to taste and continue stirring for about 5 minutes, until the dough pulls away from the sides of the pot.
  4. Cover and steam: Reduce heat to low, cover the pot tightly, and steam for 15 minutes.
  5. Serve: Uncover and stir the tuwo well before servin.

    6. Okro soup:

  6. Heat oil and saut√© aromatics: In a separate pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened. Add garlic and scotch bonnet pepper (if using) and cook for another minute.
  7. Brown the meat (optional): Add the meat pieces and cook until browned on all sides.
  8. Add tomatoes and paste: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste. Let simmer for 5 minutes to allow flavors to develop.
  9. Season and simmer: Add crayfish, thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper to taste. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  10. Garnish and serve: Enjoy your hot okro soup alongside the fluffy tuwo, garnishing with chopped parsley if desired.
